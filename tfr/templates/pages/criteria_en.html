Rating Criteria

Acting

  • Believability: Evaluates whether the actors' performances are convincing and natural, making the audience believe in their characters.
  • Chemistry and Dynamic Between Characters: Assesses how well the actors interact with each other and if their relationships on screen feel genuine and compelling.
  • Emotional Delivery: Measures the ability of the actors to convey emotions effectively, ensuring the audience can connect with the characters' feelings and experiences.

Marriage Story (2019)

Cinematography and Production

  • Composition: Looks at how well the scenes are framed and composed, including the arrangement of elements within the shot to create a visually appealing image.
  • Lighting and Color Palette: Evaluates the use of lighting to set the mood and tone, and the choice of colors to enhance the visual storytelling.
  • Camera Angles and Movements: Analyzes the use of different camera angles and movements to create dynamic and engaging scenes.
  • Sets and Props: Reviews the design and authenticity of the sets and props used to create the film's environment.
  • Makeup and Hairstyling: Considers the effectiveness of makeup and hairstyling in creating believable characters and fitting the film's setting.

Screenplay

  • Narrative Structure: Examines the overall structure of the story, including pacing, plot development, and the effectiveness of the narrative arc.
  • Originality: Considers the uniqueness and creativity of the story, characters, and plot twists.
  • Character Development: Evaluates the depth and growth of characters throughout the film, ensuring they are well-rounded and evolve in a meaningful way.
  • Dialogue Quality: Assesses the quality and naturalness of the dialogue, ensuring it enhances character development and plot progression.

Direction

  • Director style: Looks at the director's unique style and vision, and how effectively they bring the story to life.
  • Pacing: Evaluates the flow and timing of the film, ensuring scenes transition smoothly and maintain audience engagement.
  • Editing: Considers the effectiveness of editing in creating a coherent and engaging narrative, including the rhythm and pace of cuts.
  • Emotional Impact: Measures the film's ability to evoke emotions and connect with the audience on an emotional level.
  • Theme and Messages: Examines how well the film conveys its themes and messages, and whether they are thought-provoking and meaningful.
